Task 2. Validation Check
 For each idea that passes the Idea Filter, talk to 5 people in your potential target audience. Ask them:
1. Do they have the problem you’re trying to solve, and how do they currently solve it?
2. How did they explain your idea back to you? Did they understand it clearly?
3. Did they express excitement or relevance? Are they willing to pay for this solution?

Task 3. Decision Commitment Matrix
Once you've calculated the average score for each idea, use the guide below to assess your confidence level and determine your next step. This exercise will help you prioritize the ideas worth committing to, those that need further testing, and the ones you should set aside or pivot from. 
 6.0–7.0: High Confidence
You’ve received strong signals: people understand your idea, see clear value, express excitement, and show willingness to pay. 
This is your green light to fully commit. Dedicate significant time, resources, and energy to building and scaling your solution. 
5–5.9: Medium Confidence
You see some signals of interest, but responses are mixed, or there are open questions that need clarification.
Before going all in, invest time in structured experiments. Run tests over the next 60–90 days. Set clear metrics, test key assumptions, and time-box your efforts to either elevate or eliminate the idea. 
Below 5.0: Low Confidence
Feedback is lukewarm, unclear, or indicates your idea may not solve an urgent problem that people will pay to fix.
Proceed with caution. Run minimal tests or consider exploring adjacent problems and other ideas from your backlog.
